EcoTrack User Guide

Detailed breakdown of how your impact is calculated.

🚗

Gasoline Vehicle Impact

The environmental cost of driving is calculated based on the standard EPA emission factor of 0.404 kg CO2/mile. This figure accounts for the tailpipe emissions of an average passenger vehicle getting roughly 22-25 MPG, but it also considers the "well-to-wheel" impact, including the energy required to refine and transport gasoline.

Pro-Tip

Use your vehicle's trip odometer or check your weekly "Timeline" on Google Maps to find exact mileage. Idling for more than 10 seconds uses more fuel than restarting your engine, so turn it off if you're waiting!

🥩

Beef & Livestock Emissions

Beef is one of the most resource-intensive foods on the planet, requiring massive amounts of land, water, and feed. Because cattle produce methane, a greenhouse gas significantly more potent than CO2, during digestion, our tracker uses a benchmark of 6.5 kg CO2 per serving.

Visual Guide

When logging your meals, consider that a single "serving" is typically 4 to 6 ounces (115-170g). This is visually comparable to the size of a standard deck of playing cards or a typical burger patty. If you're eating a large steak (12oz+), log it as 2 or 3 servings.

⚡

Electricity Usage (kWh)

Electricity emissions are "indirect" because they happen at the power plant, not in your home. We use a national average emission factor of 0.39 kg per kWh. To find your specific data, locate your most recent utility bill and look for a section labeled "Usage Detail" or "kWh".

Where to look

For reference, 1 kWh is roughly equivalent to running a dishwasher once or keeping a laptop plugged in for 20 hours. Checking your utility bill monthly is the best way to see how seasonal changes like AC or heating affect your footprint.

✈️

Air Travel (Short vs Long Haul)

Aviation impact is categorized by distance. Short-Haul flights (< 700 miles) have high emissions per mile due to the energy required for takeoff and landing. Long-Haul flights (> 2,500 miles) use massive amounts of fuel but are slightly more efficient per mile once at cruising altitude.

Logging Accuracy

When logging, use the total miles provided by your airline ticket, not just a straight line on a map. For reference, a long-haul flight from NYC to London (3,400 miles) generates nearly 1,000 kg of CO2 per passenger.
